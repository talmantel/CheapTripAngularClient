Transportation in London

London Underground

The London Underground, also known as the Tube, is a convenient and efficient way to travel around London. With an extensive network of lines and stations, it covers most parts of the city and connects to major attractions and landmarks. The Tube operates from early morning until late at night, providing frequent service and reliable transportation for both residents and tourists.

London Buses

London buses are a popular and iconic mode of transportation in the city. The red double-decker buses are not only a convenient way to get around but also offer a scenic view of London's streets and landmarks. With an extensive bus network, you can easily reach various neighborhoods and attractions. Buses operate throughout the day and night, providing a reliable and affordable option for travelers.

Walking

Discover London on foot and see the city from a different perspective. With so many iconic landmarks and hidden gems to explore, walking is a great way to experience the city. Plus, it's completely free! Take a stroll through Hyde Park, explore the vibrant streets of Soho, or wander along the Thames. With so much to see and do, walking is a great option for budget-conscious travellers.

Docklands Light Railway

Explore London's Docklands area with the Docklands Light Railway (DLR). With over 45 stations, the DLR covers the east and south-east of London, making it easy to get around. With affordable fares and the option to use your Oyster card, travelling by DLR is a great choice for budget-conscious travellers. Don't forget to take in the stunning views of the city skyline as you ride!
